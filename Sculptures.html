Kinetic Sculpture

Kinetic art has its origin in the late 19th century. Kinetic art is a term that today most often refers to three-dimensional sculptures and figures such as mobiles that move naturally or are machine operated.

Statues

A statue is a free-standing sculpture in which the full length figures of persons or animals are carved in a durable material such as wood, metal or stone.The oldest known statue dating to about 30,000 years ago.

Relief Sculpture

The term relief refers to a sculptural method in which the sculpted pieces are bonded to a solid background of the same material. The term relief is from the Latin verb relevo, to Raise.

Bronze Sculpture

The great civilizations of the old world worked in bronze for art, from the time of the introduction of the alloy for tools and edged weapons.The Greeks were the first to scale the figures up to life size.

Ice Sculpture

Ice sculptures are generally associated with special or extravagant events.As various technologies are adapted for use with ice carving, many sculptures are now created largely by machine.

Ivory Carving

Ivory carving is the carving of ivory, that is to say animal tooth or tusk,either mechanically or manually.Ivory carving has a special importance to the medieval art of Europe and Byzantium.

Marble Sculpture

Of the many different types of marble the pure white ones are generally used for sculpture, with coloured ones preferred for many architectural and decorative uses.

Stone Sculpture

Stone carving includes a number of techniques where pieces of rough natural stone are shaped by the controlled removal of stone.Carving stone into sculpture is an activity older than civilization itself.

Wood Carving

Wood carving is a form of woodworking by means of a cutting tool in one hand and one hand on a mallet, resulting in a wooden figure.The oldest wood carved sculpture, the Shigir Idol carved from larch, is around 12,000 years old.
